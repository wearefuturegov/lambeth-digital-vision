Living in Lambeth

Lambeth residents shouldn’t have to understand how Lambeth is organised to access / receive the support they need.

Living in Lambeth brings together multiple siloed services to deliver user-centred services that cut across Lambeth’s offer.

This can save resources and ensure our efforts are focused on the most vulnerable residents who need personal support.

This will enable Lambeth to overcome:

  • The current service offer is not holistic, resulting in multiple, frustrating touchpoints

  • Unclear and disjointed communications & processes create failure demand in Civic Centre

  • Repetition of data / information collection negatively impacts the user experience

  • Number of residents increases as the number of staff remains the same

Meet Sabrina

Sabrina has just relocated to Lambeth from Southwark to move closer to her daughters school before the new term. Sabrina has a lot to organise to get her up and running. She doesn’t know Lambeth very well, and most of her friends and family live in other parts of London.

Sabrina has been unemployed for 10 months and is keen to get back into childcare. She is juggling the move, preparing her daughter for school, getting familiar with the local area, and finding a job. She’s trying to stay on top of it all.

  • Age: 35
  • Status: single
  • Ward: West Norwood
  • Motivations:
  • Career development so she can provide for her daughter
  • Work-life balance
  • Meeting new people
  • Frustrations:
  • Being overqualified for jobs
  • Dealing with social services

I move to Lambeth

Sabrina opens a welcome letter a few days after moving into her new place from Lambeth. She reads some handy information, and is prompted to text Lambeth to set up some key services.

Sabrina texts the Service Bot, and selects council tax, parking, voting and rubbish to be set up. She also selects “Get a into work” and “Find a doctor” from a list of services she might be interested in. She finds it easy to follow, and completes it all first go. She confirms the information the council already had about her, and adds additional information about her living situation.

She reads through the next steps and FAQs. She clicks on the “Get a Job” link the Service Bot has provided to get more information.

Key features

Step by step navigation - users do not have to scroll through long forms and they can see their progress, the ServiceBotwill step people through the process.

Clear next steps - users know when they have been successful and the next steps are clearly described, so the know where / how the journey will continue.

Responsive service bot - the service bot will react in kind to predetermined messaging and signpost to other services across the council and borough.

Sabrina's Outcomes

Sabrina feels empowered as she can choose her own journey, and preferred way forward.

Knowing there are a range of services to support Sabrina is comforting and reassuring.

Tailored services to her situation gives a sense of positivity that they will work for her.

How to start implementing

Design and test a responsive questionnaire to narrow down services.

Think big, start small.

Content and UX design to improve the ease of getting the right support.

Define customer service standards across Lambeth.

I thrive in Lambeth

The next day Sabrina clicks a link from an email to get her and her daughter set up at the doctor. On the same day, Sabrina opens up her council tax email. She sees when and how much her tax is, and easily pays her first installment. She clicks the link to download the payment calendar, preferring to be reminded about payment, rather than automatic payment. In her payment calendar, Sabrina turns on council tax and parking renewal reminders.

Next month, she's notified 3 days before tax is due. She opens the calendar app, however doesn't have the money currently, so selects “I think I might miss this payment”.

Sabrina is messaged 20 minutes later by the Service Bot to better understand how Lambeth can help.

Key features

Clear call to action - we make it easy for users to complete actions (like payment) and steer them towards what we would like them to do (direct debit), download payment calendar.

Payment calendar - A payment calendar you can download to nudge residents when to pay, and notify the council when it is not paid, and pay within it.

Signposting - we want people who are struggling to pay their bills to tell us as soon as possible and get support.

Sabrina's Outcomes

Sabrina is informed about clear next steps, and what she needs to do in the short term.

The payment calendar gives Sabrina the sense of financial control.

Sabrina feels at ease knowing that support is available before requiring it.

How to start implementing

Design and test new council tax email with clear signposting, next steps.

Design and test when and how to send reminders and updates during council tax process.

Test data checks across council with templates to capture data in the same way across teams.

I need support

Sabrina opens up the Service Bot text that offers relevant services to her that help avoid missing council tax. She doesn't want to speak to someone, so clicks through to the Lambeth online Advisor.

She is welcomed to the online advisor, and answers a few easy questions on her housing, health and employment situation. Sabrina is immediately presented with a few tailored options - short & long term, and services outside the council.

Sabrina likes the look of long term support services provided by the council as she wants to be independent going forward.

Key features

Online Advisor - A webpage driven advisor that asks simple questions designed to narrow down the services to access.

Other relevant services - the service will support / provide information to residents in applying for other services.

Responsive forms - designed mobile first, accessible and developed iteratively with users.

I receive support

Sabrina selects the option “Apply for tax reduction” to resolve her immediate situation in the online Advisor and easily confirms her identity. After a few extra questions she's done. The confirmation page prompts her to select a longer term option, ‘Multi-Agency support service’. Sabrina provides times to the online Advisor so it can set up an initial meeting with an advisor..

In the morning, Sabrina opens an email with confirmation of council tax reduction, and an appointment with an advisor in two days time.

The meeting goes well. She feels confident that although it’s the beginning of a long journey, the support she will receive from the “get into work” service will give her the opportunity to thrive in Lambeth. She opens her collaborative action plan in her email which sets out what her and the council will do to reach financial independence.

Key features

Online Advisor - A webpage driven advisor that asks simple questions designed to narrow down the services to access.

Multi agency support - bringing internal and external council services to the table through one channel.

Multiple channels - human or digital channels provide the same level of service.

ID authentication - picture and video identification authentication through Online Advisor

Sabrina's Outcomes

Sabrina is relieved that her problem has been resolved at first contact.

Sabrina feels organised, and can maximise her own time.

Sabrina has an increased sense of being able to be financially independent.

How to start implementing

Design and prototype confirmation emails with clear next steps and sign posting.

Prototype new forms of authentication approval.

Design and prototype email action plans that link to other services.

I stay independent

Sabrina is continuing to receive support from “get into work” as part of staying independent. Over her journey, her and her partner have moved in together, and are expecting.

After one of her check ups, Sabrina is surprised and happy to receive a congratulations message from the Service Bot. It shows her options of other services that relate to her past financial and new family status. She quickly updates her info through text, and clicks through to “Get a better start for my child”.

Sabrina gets a an occasional text from the Service Bot asking about the services she's using, and how she is. She is delighted with the joint up support she is receiving from the council and wider borough.

Key features

Responsive service bot - the service bot will react in kind to predetermined messaging, and be able to reach out to residents.

Other relevant services - the service will support / provide information to residents in applying for other services.

Signposting - we want people to know what services are available to them.

Sabrina's Outcomes

Sabrina is more knowledgeable about what is available to her given different situations.

Sabrina is confident the council has her best interest at heart.

Sabrina is reassured that support is available in this new journey.

How to start implementing

Test data checks across council with templates to capture data in the same way across teams.

Data discovery to understand any gaps in our information.

Investigate when and how to proactively reach out to residents.

I move out of Lambeth

Sabrina and her family are looking to upsize, and decide to move to Lewisham. Sabrina messages the Service Bot knowing how much she will have to do to organise the move. It responds instantly to confirm her address, and then organises final payments. It suggests a number of services that could be useful, she selects “Move my furniture”, and is shown easy access links to moving companies.

A few hours later she opens an email with her final payments, and selects to set up a payment plan rather than pay all her outstanding bills at once. Her payment calendar notifies her of the additional payments.

Sabrina enters her new postal code, and allows her and her families information to be shared with Lewisham council to make the transition as smooth as possible.

Key features

Other relevant services - the service will support / provide information to residents in applying for other services.

Clear next steps - users know when they have been successful and the next steps are clearly described.

Data sharing - open data standards to be able to share between boroughs, and any other community services that the resident might like.

Sabrina's Outcomes

Sabrina feels relaxed about the move seeing all the information she needs is at her fingertips.

The payment plan gives Sabrina freedom to thrive faster in her new home.

Knowing Sabrina’s next borough has her information give time back to Sabrina to thrive faster in her new home.

How to start implementing

Design and prototype a leaving message for residents.

Design and prototype a single leaving bill, including payment plan.

Investigate possibility of data sharing with other boroughs.
