Support people into work

Three concepts that bring to life what a collaborative approach of supporting people into work could look like in a digitally enabled future.

Lambeth is changing and growing fast, as more people choose to come to the borough to live, work and study.

The proportion of residents claiming out-of-work benefits remains above London and national averages. By far the largest group of claims are for health reasons.

  • Approx 11,500 Lambeth residents were unemployed in the year to June 2018

  • Despite improvement in wage levels in-work poverty remains an important issue

  • Lambeth has one of the highest rates of unemployment among 50-64 year olds in London

Meet Reuben

Reuben does not have a lot of formal education, but he prides himself in being a quick learner, working his way to middle management. It's been nearly two years since he was made redundant. His confidence has decreased and he's struggling to make ends meet for his family. This has had a significant impact on his mental well-being and relationships. Reuben wants to take control of his life and get the right support.

  • Age: 42
  • Status: married with 3 children
  • Ward: Coldharbour
  • Motivations:
  • Supporting his family
  • Spending time with his family
  • Being independent and feeling useful
  • Frustrations:
  • Signing-on weekly
  • Zero hour contracts
  • Increasing mental health challenges

Concept 1 - Making the right connections

A platform centered on connecting people to people, not jobs. Supporting residents on their journey to employment by creating opportunities to develop a diverse network of community and peer support built on relationships.

Reuben's Story

After a recent social housing assessment, the housing advisor suggests Reuben explores the new making the right connections service, to connect to the right employment support.

Reuben logs on and creates a profile. He schedules a video call with the Employment Adviser (Amy). She suggests a local group led by Dads, thinking it will be a great way to build his confidence. Reuben agrees and messages the organiser of the local group to find out more.

Reuben wants to work on his CV, so uses the ‘get feedback’ function on the platform which allows him to submit a CV to a local volunteer. Two days later he receives a notification and message from volunteer, providing feedback and an offer to have a video call to talk it through.

He attends the local group for Dads and likes it so much he sign-ups for the next session. He gives the group 4 stars and leaves a recommendation.

Key features

Online advisor and calendar

Submit CV which can be reviewed by local professionals

A forum where you can get advice from others about career or local opportunities (with featured success stories)

The ability directly message programme leads and those attending workshops

Reuben's Outcomes

Greater control of his employment journey: Easy access to Advisors and the ability to make new connections to people means Reuben's able to access a range of support around his life and family responsibilities. This enables him to have greater control over his journey to employment.

Increased confidence & wider network: Reuben is able to build new relationships with others in the community developing his confidence and widening the network of people that can offer support.

A stronger connection to the community: Increased connections with those in the community widens his professional network and gives him access to job opportunities. A deeper connection to the support found in Lambeth, so he knows he’s not alone.

Concept 2 - Support Hub

A vibrant place for residents to access complementary formal and informal support, information and guidance and have on the spot referrals.

Reuben's Story

With his improved CV Reuben gets selected for more interviews, but unfortunately nothing materialises. He feels low and discouraged. He heads to the employment hub which delivers advice and support services in one place. He is met by an Adviser (Ben) who is able bring up his profile and understand Reuben's employment journey and goals.

Together they create a plan of the people and organisations he can connect with. Using a shared calendar, Ben can see available times. He books an appointment for Reuben and an interview techniques and confidence buidling course. In between the two sessions Reuben attends a peer-network delivered by those who have experienced long-term employment but had started small businesses.

Key features

Joint community events

Data - open source referrals between organisations

Information is shared between organisations and more strategic support can be planned

Drop in courses and training provided between appointments

Reuben's Outcomes

Increased optimism: Having access to a variety of connected services gives Reuben a more connected experience and clarity on the diversity of support he can use to move forward.

Increased well-being: having a diverse and impactful experience at the hub, gives him an increased sense of well-being, connection and energy on his journey to well-being.

Increased confidence, skills, knowledge and connections: The courses, session and conversations he has with others builds Reuben's knowledge about local support, and opportunities for him to learn and develop new skills.

Concept 3 - Local careers programme

A programme for local residents to gain flexible work experience experience, training, and development from a collection of local businesses each hosting a part of the programme.

Reuben's Story

One week later Reuben joins a cohort to start a programme focused on mentoring and coaching which he enjoys. By the time Reuben moves on to his third company rotation, his confidence around securing a job has increased and he knows what he would like to explore.

At the end of the programme Reuben is put into a pool of candidates who can apply for roles at the companies involved and receives a local accreditation. Applying the learning he gained over the last 3 months, he completes 3 applications with 2 of the companies offering him a job. 1 year on, Reuben is working at a Housing Association as a Training Officer optimistic about the future.

Key features

Local authority providing a central HR function to coordinate programme and build capabilities

Local businesses involved in the programme awarded a ‘gold star’

Residents on the programme receive a local accreditation

Reuben's Outcomes

Increased confidence: new exposure to the working environment and getting familiar again with being employed and managed, Reuben no longer doubts he will be up to the pace and expectations, especially with the right support.

Developing new skills: Through the training Reuben rediscovers things that he is a good at, but also learn new skills and ways of working.

Clearer sense of direction: Working in three local businesses has made is clearer what Rueben what he wants to enjoy, the value he can add and direction he wants to go in.

What did we learn about the digital future of Lambeth services?

Services and solutions will be centred on strengthening relationships, community assests and networks

People will have increasingly interconnected relationships with each other and this will be reflected in support and services are delivered.

Mobilising civic action & communities delivering services

New opportunities for local residents to volunteer their skills and support. Local communities will play a crucial role in shaping and delivering services.

A focus on 'good work'

Business, local organisations and the council will focus on how people can pursue their aspirations and play to their strengths, enabling them to find employment that is right for them.

Internal testing & learning

Lambeth internal teams and departments will be using opportunities to test and learn about what works in supporting the community, using this knowledge to influence local businesses and organisations.

Increased collaboration and coordination

Local organisations will be working closely together, with shared outcomes, objectives and outreach; maximising their collective knowledge and impact. Lambeth with play a role of convening online and offline spaces for learning to be shared between organistations using their influence to create strategic opportunities for the sector.

Place-based approaches

Services will be shaped based on characteristics of different localities, building on local assets and recognising the nuances between communities. There is no one-size fits all approach.

Businesses working closely with communities and each other

Businesses of all sizes will be active in their communities economic and social development, with a focus on developing initiatives for their exisiting employees to support the local community. Also business working more closely with other local businesses (big and small) to take a more joined up approach to corporate social responsibility.
