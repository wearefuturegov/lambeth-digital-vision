Support people into work

Three concepts that bring to life what a collaborative approach of supporting people into work could look like in a digitally enabled future.

Lambeth is changing and growing fast, as more people choose to come to the borough to live, work and study.

The proportion of residents claiming out-of-work benefits remains above London and national averages. By far the largest group of claims are for health reasons.

  • Approx 11,500 Lambeth residents were unemployed in the year to June 2018

  • Despite improvement in wage levels in-work poverty remains an important issue

  • Lambeth has one of the highest rates of unemployment among 50-64 year olds in London


Meet Reuben

Reuben does not have a lot of formal education, but he prides himself in being a quick learner, working his way to middle management. It's been nearly two years since he was made redundant. His confidence has decreased and he's struggling to make ends meet for his family. This has had a significant impact on his mental well-being and relationships. Reuben wants to take control of his life and get the right support.

  • Age: 42
  • Status: married with 3 children
  • Ward: Coldharbour
  • Motivations:
  • Supporting his family
  • Spending time with his family
  • Being independent and feeling useful
  • Frustrations:
  • Signing-on weekly
  • Zero hour contracts
  • Increasing mental health challenges

Concept 1 - Making the right connections

A platform centred on connecting people to people, not to jobs. It will support residents on their employment journey by creating opportunities to create a diverse network of community and peer support, built on relationships.

Reuben's Story

After a social housing assessment, the housing advisor suggests the ‘making the right connections’ service, to get Reuben the right employment support.

Reuben logs on, creates a profile and schedules a video call with an Employment Adviser (Amy). She suggests a local group of Dads, to build his confidence. Reuben look at the revies, and messages the organiser of the local group to find out more.

Reuben uses the ‘get feedback’ function on the platform to submit his CV to a local volunteer for feedback. Two days later he receives feedback, and the opportunity to talk it through the feedback via video call.

Key features

Online advisor and calendar

Submit CV which can be reviewed by local professionals

A forum where you can get advice from others about career or local opportunities (with featured success stories)

The ability directly message programme leads and those attending workshops

Reuben's Outcomes

Greater control of his employment journey
Reuben can access a range of support alongside his life and family responsibilities. Advisors and support groups create new connections during his journey to employment.

Increased confidence & wider network
Reuben is able to build new relationships with others in the community, building his confidence and network of support.

A stronger connection to the community
More connections and a larger network gives him access to job opportunities. A feels a deeper connection to the support found in Lambeth, so he knows he’s not alone.

Concept 2 - Support Hub

A vibrant place for residents to access formal and informal support, that all compliment one another to provide information, guidance and give on the spot referrals.

Reuben's Story

Reuben gets selected for more interviews with his new CV, but nothing comes from them. He feels discouraged. He heads to the employment hub which delivers support services all in one place. He is met by an Adviser (Ben) who pulls up Reuben’s profile and reads though his employment journey and goals.

They create a plan of the people and organisations to connect with. Using a shared calendar, Ben books an appointment for Reuben and an ‘interview techniques’ and ‘confidence building’ course. Between the courses Reuben attends a peer-network delivered by those who have successfully been on his employment journey.

Key features

Joint community events

Data - open source referrals between organisations

Information is shared between organisations and more strategic support can be planned

Drop in courses and training provided between appointments

Reuben's Outcomes

Increased optimism
The access to a variety of services gives Reuben a more connected experience. He leaves with clarity on the diversity of support he can use.

Increased well-being
The diverse and impactful experience at the hub gives Reuben an increased sense of well-being, connection and energy.

Increased confidence, skills, knowledge and connections
The courses, session and conversations he has at the hub builds Reubens knowledge about local support. He leaves knowing opportunities exist to learn and develop new skills.

Concept 3 - Local careers programme

A programme for local residents to gain flexible work experience, training and development from a collection of local businesses who each host different parts of the programme.

Reuben's Story

One week later Reuben joins a cohort to start a programme focused on mentoring and coaching. By the time Reuben moves on to his third company rotation, his confidence to secure a job has increased and he knows what he would like to explore.

At the end of the programme Reuben can apply for roles at the companies involved in the programme, and receives an accreditation of training and experience. His learnings over the last 3 months allows him to complete 3 applications, 2 of the companies offer him a job.

One year on, Reuben is working at a Housing Association as a Training Officer optimistic about the future.

Key features

Local authority providing a central HR function to coordinate programme and build capabilities

Local businesses involved in the programme awarded a ‘gold star’

Residents on the programme receive a local accreditation

Reuben's Outcomes

Increased confidence
Exposure to the working environment, and getting re-familiarised with being employed removes doubts in Reuben’s mind. He now thinks he can meet expectations with the right support.

Developing new skills
Through the training, Reuben rediscovers things that he is a good at. He also learns new skills and ways of working.

Clearer sense of direction
Working in three local businesses has made it clearer to Reuben what he enjoys. He feels like he is bringing value to his community, and is doing what he likes while doing it.

What did we learn about the digital future of Lambeth services?

Services will be centred on strengthening relationships, community assets and networks

People will have increasingly connected relationships with each other, this will be reflected in the support and services delivered.

Mobilising civic action, and communities delivering services

There will be opportunities for residents to volunteer their skills and support. Local communities are crucial in shaping and delivering future services.

A focus on 'good work'

Business, local organisations and the council will focus on supporting people to pursue aspirations, and play to strengths. Enabling them to find employment that is right for them.

Internal testing & working

Lambeth council’s teams and departments will use opportunities to test and learn about what works best to support the community.

Increased collaboration & coordination

Local organisation will work closely together with shared outcomes and outreach to maximise their collective impact. The council will convene online and offline spaces for learnings to be shared across the borough, using their influence to create strategic opportunities.

Place-based approaches

Services will be shaped based on characteristics of different localities, building on local assets and recognising the differences between communities. The is no one-size fits all.

Businesses working closely with communities and each other

Businesses of all sizes will be active in their communities economic and social development, with a focus on developing initiatives for their exisiting employees to support the local community. Also business working more closely with other local businesses (big and small) to take a more joined up approach to corporate social responsibility.
