Delivering on our vision

To deliver our vision, it is broken down into three digital themes and outcomes. They focus on transforming how we support our residents, use our data, and work as a council to remain relevant in the 21st century.

End-to-end service experience

Making it easier to engage with us digitally

  • Offer improved experiences that meet the raised expectations of our customers by redesigning services across Lambeth

  • Use technology to help meet the demand put on our services, and ensure focus is in the right areas

  • Create seamless digital experiences that work across teams and departments, avoid frustration and delay, and keep customers informed

  • Integrate systems and data, and automate repetitive business processes so staff can concentrate on work that adds value

  • Make digital payments seamless, easy and accessible in every area

  • Ensure that technology and digital standards are in place and that partners and suppliers adhere to them

Data driven organisation

Using the information we have to understand what people want and need, to define delivery priorities

  • Integrate, manage and use data as an asset that informs the ongoing improvement of services and customer experiences

  • Use data and intelligence to design more effective and preventative service models

  • Develop on-demand management information that gives insight into spend patterns and pressures

  • Use data from across the borough to support innovation and new service developments for our residents and businesses

  • Ensure data quality, maintenance, privacy & security are an organisational and partnership priority by design

  • Seek opportunities to share and publish data openly where it will help to better meet the needs of our customers

Agile ways of working

Challenge & change the norms of how we work, removing barriers to innovation & meeting the public’s expectation

  • Enable the creation of an agile, highly skilled and productive workforce that can deliver quality services at pace

  • Align how we govern, procure, manage risk and deliver with agile ways of working

  • Invest in technology, tools and the people to build the capability needed to be responsive to challenges faced by the organisation now and in the future

  • Use technology to improve the productivity and effectiveness of our workforce, enabling mobile working to deliver highly responsive services

  • Ensure our digital vision and priorities are reflected in our policies and strategies across the organisation

To deliver these outcomes, we will deliver small improvements often. Instead of planning the 5 year implementation, let’s:

  • Invest in people first - not technology
  • Understand user need & work in an agile way
  • Focus on priority areas of Lambeth
  • Start small, prove we can deliver

This means, building internal capacity to run the programme in an agile way - and deliver benefits early and often

So, to get started on our vision we will


  • Get senior leader buy in - educate on digital, design, agile, and identify priority areas to transform and / or fix

  • Increase the capacity and capability of current IT / Digital team (design, data, tech, delivery - decision to be made on model / makeup of team)

  • Adopt and embed GDS design, data and technology standards (local gov digital declaration)

  • Establish and resource a Digital Transformation programme to plan and deliver the change

  • Prioritise and fix things that need to be as a result of step 1 and ongoing work

  • User-centred design of a priority holistic service, and a focussed service to inform technology and data choices

  • Case for Change that includes user needs, costs, and benefits (tech and data included)
